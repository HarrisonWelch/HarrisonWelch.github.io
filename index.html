Celestial-Inertial UAV Navigation in a GPS-negative Environment

Updated: 6/27/2018

Developed a system for an unmanned aerial vehicle or drone to locate itself, without the use of GPS, on earth based on the position of the stars directly above the drone using technologies such as the Python programming language, Computer Vision, Astrophotography, and Parrot AR remote control quad-copter. We mounted a highly sensitive ZWO ASI 178MM astrophotography camera to a Parrot AR drone aimed straight upwards. The camera takes a photo of the stars at night. Then using Computer Vision coupled with an open-source star analysis algorithm called "Astrometry.net" we pick out all seen stars giving our location on the celestial sphere. Utilizing US Navy algorithms, we convert the celestial Right Ascension and Declination into the drones GPS location, accurate to a few miles.
