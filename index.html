Celestial-Inertial UAS Navigation in a GPS-negative Environment

Updated: 6/17/2018

Auburn REU in Smart UAVs
Abstract: In research, communicating results is a fundamental task. Communicating can today take multiple forms: technical reports, papers, articles, PowerPoint presentations, or videos. Writing remains the pinnacle of communicating and reporting research activities. A paper, a thesis, or a report all will include these following components: an abstract, an introduction, a body, and a conclusion. The abstract, the introduction, and the conclusion share these common elements: the context, a problem description, references to the state of art and best solutions so far, the key/rationale of your solution, the methods used to evaluate your solution, idea, or contribution, and finally a conclusion announcing the key results. In short, these components will have the same structure and contain the same information. They differ only by the extent of detail.
